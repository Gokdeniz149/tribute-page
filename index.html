NICOLA TESLA

The Man Who Illuminated the World

Here's a time line of Nicola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born on July 10th in Smiljan, Croatia (then part of the Austrian Empire)
  • 1875 - Enrolled at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, Austria to study electrical engineering
  • 1881 - Moved to Budapest and worked for the Central Telegraph Office
  • 1882 - Developed the concept of the rotating magnetic field, which is the basis for most AC machinery
  • 1884 - Emigrated to the United States and began working for Thomas Edison in New York City
  • 1887 - Patented the induction motor and other devices that used alternating current (AC)
  • 1891 - Became a naturalized citizen of the United States and invented the Tesla coil, which is still used in radio technology today
  • 1893 - Demonstrated wireless transmission of electricity at the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago
  • 1899 - Conducted experiments in Colorado Springs, where he claimed to have received signals from outer space
  • 1901-1905 - Worked on the Wardenclyffe Tower project, an early wireless transmission station, but it was never completed due to financial difficulties
  • 1917 - The Wardenclyffe Tower was demolished during World War I for its scrap metal
  • 1934 - Awarded the Edison Medal by the American Institute of Electrical Engineers for his contributions to electrical engineering
  • 1943 - Died on January 7th in New York City at the age of 86. Despite his many contributions to science and technology, he died penniless and alone.
